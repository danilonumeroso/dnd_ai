## Turn 6

### Dungeon Master

Seyrie grips her sword hilt tightly and begins her descent down the narrow stone staircase. The steps are worn smooth by centuries of use, each one slick with moisture. Alfinn follows close behind, his holy symbol glowing faintly with a soft, comforting light that pushes back the immediate darkness. Thalindra brings up the rear, her staff clenched in both hands, eyes scanning the shadows. Aerion, ever the scout, melts into the gloom beside the staircase, his movements silent as he keeps watch above and below.

The air grows colder and damper with each step, the sound of dripping water growing louder—a steady rhythm echoing from unseen depths. After about twenty feet, the staircase opens into a small, circular chamber carved from the same cold stone. In the center stands a pedestal of rough-hewn rock, upon which rests a small, leather-bound book, its cover emblazoned with a sigil of a three-toed claw. The book seems to pulse with a faint, inner light.

As the party enters the chamber, a soft, wet clicking sound comes from the shadows in the far wall—something small and many-legged skittering away into darkness.
